to Your Time Away From Time

The dim nicotine haze gives all the lights a diffused, brightly colored halo. Through the smoke-filled air, the Bar is much larger than it appeared from the outside, though it's too dark to really tell. Supertramp plays softly from a glowing jukebox in a rear corner that looks vertiginously far away.



It's easy to miss him at first, the Bartender standing there with his back to the door.



The reflection of his hands in the mirror show they match the rest of him: creased, well-worn. He's washing a tall beer glass and then dries it with an equally threadbare towel, before placing the glass among a stack of others just like it, a crystal castle. He never looks up from his work, but he can sense a new presence in his space, a new planet in his orbit.



When he turns, his eyes are piercing blue and his voice sounds like the dry, rocky landscape that surrounds this place.





"What can I do you for?"
