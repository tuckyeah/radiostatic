June Variable

I have been wandering in the desert for so long now I am beginning to feel like Moses, though I fear my direction is not quite as clear.

I can't recall what brought us here. I can't recall why we started on this investigation. I can barely remember the day we first arrived here.

I can only trust in the movement of my feet carrying me forwards.

My memories are a castle made of sand, and the tide is coming in.

Since Charlie has been gone, I have found myself adrift.

I have felt his absence as an acute darkness in my life. Being near him is like the white hot heat of the sun, and I fear I may have become dependent on it - and, by extension, on him - for my survival.

I cannot explain it, I just know I am being pulled towards my inexorable destination, the way a compass needle must feel when it is pulled to the magnetic pole.

There are no footprints along the way, no sign of human life. The sand is completely undisturbed for miles around.

My ears play tricks on me in the vast silence of the basin, and I catch bits and pieces of whispers on the wind. I worry I am losing my mind, I worry I am more like that Old Man than I realized.

As if my fears had been heard, I catch my first glimpse of other human existence:

more papers, bright white and new, half buried in the sand. It seems Leopold did come here after all.
