June Variable

I have spent so long walking in this desert I am beginning to feel like Moses, though I am not sure my purpose is quite as clear.

Since Charlie has been gone, I have found myself adrift. I can't even recall when I started walking.

My memories are a castle made of sand along the shore, and now the tide is coming in.

I do not expect anyone else to understand why I follow Charlie, or why I trust in him so wholeheartedly. I can't say I believe everything he says, or even fully understand what it is exactly he believes.

But being near him is like the white hot heat of the sun, and I fear I may have become dependent on it - and, by extension, on him - for my survial.

There are no footprints along the way, no sign of human life. The sand completely undisturbed.

I couldn't even find our own tracks from the other day (wasn't it just a day or so ago that we walked here together?)

Nevertheless, even with nothing to lead me, I found myself pulled towards my inexorable destination, the way a compass needle must feel when it is pulled to the magnetic pole.

But instead of Charlie, all I find is more papers, half-buried in the sand.
