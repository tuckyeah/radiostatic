June Variable

I have spent so long wandering in this desert I am beginning to feel like Moses, though I am not sure my purpose is quite as clear. Since Charlie has been gone, I have found myself adrift. I can't even recall when I started walking.

My memories are a castle made of sand along the shore, and now the tide is coming in.

I headed to the Satellites, my last hope to find Charlie. There were still no footprints along the way, the sand completely undisturbed. I couldn't even find our own tracks from the other day (wasn't it just a day or so ago that we walked here together?)

Though even with nothing lead me, I found the field with no problems. I couldn't shake the sensation I was pulled here somehow, the way a compass needle must feel when it is pulled to the magentic pole.

The beasts stood silent, still; the dishes pointed at the stars like hundreds of searching sightless eyes. I stood watching them back until I saw a fluttering movement out of the corner of my eye.


Paper. Another page from the od man's journals. Perhaps he had come here after all.
