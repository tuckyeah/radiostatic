June Variable

What was I to make of these letters? I could not explain them, and that gave me a sickly cold feeling in my chest.

That was my name, Charlie's name, and though they described someone similar to me, they were telling a story I had never heard before -- and certainly never experienced.

At first, I thought maybe these were new writings by the old man. Something he had jotted down recently, inspired by our meeting.

But the pages were sun faded and yellowed, much too old to have been written in the few days since Charlie and I had arrived at this place.

Hands trembling, I did the only thing I could and went back to the Bar.

"You've seen us before, my partner and I," I demanded. "Have we ever come in here before? Either separately or together?"

I wasn't sure what I was asking or looking for; I wasn't sure of anything anymore.

The Bartender stared at me evenly, as if considering me for the first time, sizing me up. "Nope, he said slowly, after allowing a long pause to pass. Though if I had, would that change anything?

Of course it would change things - it would change everything. But I couldn't explain how or why, I couldn't quite articulate what I meant and this surprised me.

A thousand half-thought ideas, phrases, raced through my head at the speed of light but I couldn't hold on to any of them.

They crumbled and disappeared as soon as my focus landed on them, leaving me standing there with my mouth open, poised to speak but with nothing to say. I pursed my lips.

"Maybe he's the one you're looking for," the Bartender said at last, with a surprising tenderness. "Or maybe he's looking for you. There's so many possibilities if you'll let yourself consider them..."

"But none of them are real! Or possible!" I exclaimed, feeling the world get lighter and brighter around me.

"How much does it really matter which one is 'right' or 'real'?" He said in that same even tone.

"Just because something is only in your head... does that make it any less real? Any less meaningful, important, valuable? What does it truly change?"

Before I could respond, I felt my knees give way beneath me as I collapsed to the floor.
