CHARLIE WAITS

It didn't take long to find the photo of her license plate from tollbooth's database of pictures, and from there it was just a hop, skip and a jump to acquiring her name and address.

June Variable

was her name.

It tasted like home on my tongue.

Over the next few weeks, I followed her and learned everything I possibly could about this woman I loved.

She works in a morgue; she takes her coffee with two creams and no sugar. She spends her days at work and her nights alone in her studio apartment with her cat, Mr. Peebles, and a well-worn copy of Moby Dick.

Her father was a naval captain and her mother died when she was only a girl. She was a fierce follower of conspiracy theories of all varieties, but loved the ones about Lizard People from Space the most. She stopped at the 7-11 once a week on her way home from work to pick up a stack of tabloids, which she pored over that evening at her kitchen table.

She's never been married, no kids, and she sleeps with a 9mm Glock under her pillow.

The more I discovered, the more perfect she became to me.

Even her flaws were beautiful.

This terrified me.
