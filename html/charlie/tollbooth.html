It was around three in the morning and I was working the graveyard shift like always. The booth window was open and a cool southern breeze carried the scent of blood and flesh from the meat processing plant a ways down the road. I had my radio turned up and I was listening carefully for the first notes of "Nights in White Satin."

I remember because Late Night Lionel from WTPX had given me a hot tip about a cash prize for the first caller after that song played. I'd gotten to be good friends with most of the local late-night DJs and they'd throw me a bone every now and then about some station contests - the odd Doors boxed-CD set or a station T-Shirt autographed by the members of the morning show.

I never won any of the big ticket stuff - even this cash prize was a long shot - but it didn't bother me none. I was never meant to be a big ticket winner anyway.

I looked up at the road and saw two headlights heading towards me, like eyes boring into mine.

My entire body broke out in goosebumps even though it was the middle of the summer, but not from fear - from excitement, anticipation. By the time the car slowed to pass through the gate, my heart was pounding so hard I could feel it in my finger tips.

Then the song started and I swear time stopped.

She looked just like she did in my dreams: red hair, sensible pantsuit, wire-rim glasses, just a little bit of makeup to look like she was wearing none at all. She tossed her quarter in the basin and looked right at me.

That's when I realized I was gaping open-mouthed at her. Before I could gather myself and my manners, she gave me the warmest, most comforting smile I have ever received from another human being, and drove off into the night.

This had to be some kind of sign, some gift from the universe, right?

I mean, what are the odds?
