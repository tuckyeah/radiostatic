I know this sounds crazy, but the first time I met June was in a dream.

Well, I guess if we're being specific... it was more like a series of dreams.

Over the course of three, maybe four months.

So imagine my surprise when one day, just walking to my job, I passed her on the street.

Her.

Really her. In the flesh and blood, and just as beautiful as I'd always imagined. I swear my heart almost stopped when she walked right by me (though of course she didn't see me. That would have been too much to hope for.)

This had to be some kind of sign, some gift from the universe. I mean, what are the odds? Immediately, I called my boss from my cellphone and faked a family emergency (the nice thing about having no family is no one can check up on whether or not its true) and I took off after her.

I followed her for weeks.

I learned she works in a morgue, she takes her coffee with two creams and no sugar. She spends her days at work and nights alone in her apartment with her cat and a well-worn copy of Moby Dick.

Her contact prescription is -4.75 x -4.5 and she has a slight astigmatism. Her father was a naval captain and her mother died when she was only a girl.

She has never been married, no kids, and sleeps with a gun next to her on the bedside table.

Were we star-crossed lovers or what?

Everything was going so well. I'd been working on a plan to finally meet her. I was going to wait til a Friday afternoon when I'll pick her up in my van. I'd take her to this little cabin in the woods that's been in my family for years. I've got it all decorated with pictures I've taken of her. In some of them I've pasted myself in, so she'll see how great we look together.

I figured she'd be upset at first, but once she gives me the chance to talk to her, once I explain everything to her, she'll see that we're meant to be together. She'd fall in love with me just like I had fallen in love with her.

Then she disappeared.
