As I stood there, I let my eyes drift across the shapes of constellations and I traced them with my tongue on the roof of my mouth - Orion, Cassiopeia, the Big Dipper.

Could June see the stars, from wherever she might be? Was she counting the same constellations, or were hers somehow different - at another angle, from another perspective, another sky entirely?

Once, in a dream, she had chided me for being such a shut-in. "Don't you get bored?" she had demanded. She was right, of course, but now I hoped that maybe her disappearence was all aprt of an elaborate plan to get me out of my house and into the world. I knew it was a selfish notion, but hope springs eternal.

A meteorite sliced through Orion's Belt and flamed out spectacularly above me. I thought of how things like that burned up on entry, and how its molecules would become part of our atmosphere, and how long would it take for those space molecules to make their way into my lungs...

when the radio crackled into life.
