CHARLIE WAITS

I quit my job, cashed out my savings, and started driving west. I drove as far as I could before my car gave out and it breathed its last breath in a cloud of white steam that quickly disappeared into the hot air.

I checked under the hood and confirmed that the duct tape on the radiator had finally, at last, given up the ghost, leaving me stranded in the middle of the desert. I hadn't seen a car, much less a building, in hours.

But just over the horizon, a bright pink glowing caught the corner of my eye.

"What can I do you for?" asked the Bartender when I entered. For a moment, I forgot how to speak. It had been so long since I had needed to use my voice.


"Do you have a radio?" I croaked.


He considered me a moment, sizing me up. I remember noticing how bright blue his eyes were when he asked me.

"How long has she been missing?"

"What?" I managed, nearly too stunned for words, but he just gave me a smile and the old radio from the shelf behind him.



"You just make sure to bring that on back whenever you're done searching the skies, Charlie."

I walked out to my car, flipped the radio on and set it on the hood, and then I proceeded to do just what I was named for...



and Wait.
