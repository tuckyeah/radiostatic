On the third day, he became worried she had died somewhere in the apartment, some hallway or nook that he couldn’t see from any of his vantage points.

So, he put on his old UPS uniform, the one he still kept in the trunk of his car for this very purpose, and jimmied open the lock on her apartment door.

Success

it had been months since he’d been here. Immediately he knew he was alone though, even from outside the door. A dead body gets a very particular smell surprisingly quickly and the apartment still smelled fresh, like someone had just closed a window.

It still smelled of her, too - faintly of formaldehyde and cotton-scented deodorant. He inhaled deeply and felt at once at home.

Okay, so then where was she?

Her sheets were as she’d left them - as if she’d just climbed out of bed. This made him stop in his tracks, for Charlie had never seen her leave the house without making her bed. She made a note of it each morning in her routine, often smiling to herself as she tucked the sheets under her two pillows.

Now they lay tousled, rumpled and it struck him as vaguely - and somehow wrongly - intimate. He had never looked at her naked, no matter how much he had ever wanted to. Suddenly he felt shamed, embarrassed, like he was invading some part of her life he was never meant to see.

His face grew hot and his ears burned and he turned and ran out the door, barely remembering to lock it behind him.

Coward.
