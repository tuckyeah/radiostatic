CHARLIE WAITS

I sat in my car until the sun went down and the stars came out, just staring at the horizon and trying to figure out what to do. I was out of ideas.

What else could I do? Call the police? Hah, what would they say? I didn't actually know her, not personally anyway. We've never spoken a word to one another, and I doubt she could pick me out of a crowd. Besides, I can't imagine they would understand how I came about all this information anyway.

And yet it seemed increasingly clear that I knew her better than anyone else on the planet, and even more likely that I was the only person who had noticed she was missing at all. No matter how much I tried to come up with an alternative, I couldn't ignore the obvious solution:

If I ever wanted to see June again, I would have to be the one to find her.
