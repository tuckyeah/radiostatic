This great basin was once part of a great sea, and these dishes are remnants of seashells. Put an ear up to them and listen: voices of people hidden in the static, voices long forgotten or never heard at all. TV jingles, radio ads, Nixon's resignation, a mother whispering goodnight in her child's ear. They're all crashing at once in this hushed ocean of sound.

Our bodies are made of water and the hearts of dying stars.
