The cave rustled with the susurrus of voices. Hundreds of them, just too quite or distant to decipher but still flowing with the same beautiful cadence of human spech. I heard snippets of music too, thunderous applaus, the canned roar of a laugh track. Or at least I thought I could hear these things - it was like making shapes from static, seeing faces in the clouds.



I turned my face upwards, to where I had fallen from, but to my surprise I saw a thousand thousand thousand holes across the sky, just like the one I had fallen from. I saw constellations, the lines of connection between us and the vast empty spaces that divide us. They were not holes but stars, and I was moving among them, another infinitesimal dot in the heavens, part of everything and yet miles apart and drifting alone through space and time. I thoguht of how we used to tell him, our Sweet Child, that stars were nothing but holes to heaven.
