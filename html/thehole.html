The Hole.

The pipe is easy to miss, buried wrist-high in a mound of sand. Its dark corroded metal mouth, about fist-wide, still holds warmth from the sun, making it feel like flesh to the touch. Like it could plausibly have a pulse. The air that comes from within is stale and smells of time, pulp, and dust. It carries the sounds of a thousand whispers and heartbeats. The pipe leads to nowhere, flowing only to a wooden box another few inches below the surface, equally crammed full of papers.



Some of these pages are yellowed, cream-colored with age, and others are still bright white like stars. Some are tucked in enevelopes, some bound in journals, some just loose leaves that could blow away across the basin and disappear forever. The older ones near the bottom are damp, soft and spongy, molecules clinging together against the pull of nature and decay.
