Way out in the middle-western of America, on one of those old highways that have long since fallen to the wayside since the advent of the Interstate,


there's this Bar.



From the outside, it doesn't look like much: an old silver Airstream trailer hooked up to a generator and wrapped in pink Christmas lights.

There's nothing else for miles around but trees and fields,


trees and fields,


trees and fields.









Or so you'd think.

In broad daylight, the horizon twinkled with glittering stars - rays of sunlight bouncing off the field of satellites.







To the east sits the skeleton of a ranch house,
taken to peeling and fading in
the relentless sunlight.






But let's be honest,
